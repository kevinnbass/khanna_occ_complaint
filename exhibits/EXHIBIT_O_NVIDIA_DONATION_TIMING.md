### 1.1 Disclosure-universe scope: the parents-in-law tranche is outside § 13104(a)

For complete clarity, the disclosure-violation theory under 5 U.S.C. § 13104(a) reaches only the Member, the Member's spouse, and the Member's dependent children. The 7,255-share Monte / Usha Ahuja Family Trust tranche reflects giving by the Member's parents-in-law, which is not reportable on the Member's Annual Personal Financial Disclosure under any sub-section of § 13104(a) and is not pleaded as a non-disclosure violation in any Count of the operative complaint. The 2,821-share Ritu Ahuja Khanna tranche, by contrast, is the Member's spouse's giving and sits squarely within § 13104(d)(1)(A)'s spousal reach for purposes of any Schedule G charitable-contribution audit by the Committee on Ethics. The Foundation-grant chain documented elsewhere in the package — and the donation-timing × Congressional-event overlay developed in §§ 2–4 below — is offered as conflict-of-interest pattern evidence under House Rule XXIII clause 3 and 18 U.S.C. § 208 framing, not as a § 13104(a) non-disclosure pleading at the parents-in-law layer.

## 2. Date-resolution gap (negative finding on per-day overlay)

IRS Form 990-PF Schedule B Non-Cash Property Contribution group does not require a date-of-receipt for individual contributions. The parsed XML's `date` field is null for every TY2024 NVIDIA tranche. Form 990-PF instructions require contributor name, address, and aggregate annual contribution by type (cash versus non-cash) by tax year — not per-event dates. The filed paper 990-PF may carry handwritten or tabular date detail not reflected in the e-file XML.

**Implication.** A per-day overlay against specific Congressional events is not possible from the machine-readable Schedule B data. The defensible temporal posture establishes (a) the Foundation tax year (TY2024 = CY2024) and (b) the universe of NVIDIA-relevant Congressional events the subject participated in during that 12-month window, then positions the donations within that 12-month frame. A future enrichment from the original paper 990-PF PDF could close the gap.

The Building Chips in America Act is the 2024 House calendar's NVIDIA-relevant vehicle: its purpose is to expand domestic semiconductor production by streamlining federal permitting and providing additional incentives for chipmaking facilities. ## 4. Stop-criteria evaluation

The originating analysis specified that, if the donation date is more than 30 days from any NVIDIA-relevant Congressional event and from any NVIDIA 8-K material item, a negative finding is filed. Result: the donation date is null in the source data, so the strict ±30-day test cannot be performed against a specific calendar day.

**Disposition.** The date-null gap is preserved as an auditable limitation. Separately, the TY2024 calendar context is documented: the year in which the household moved $6,285,675 to the foundation — including 10,076 NVIDIA shares reported at $1,667,345 donation-time fair market value on IRS Form 990-PF Schedule B — is the same year the subject voted NAY on the Building Chips in America Act, voted YEA on four China-policy bills, executed at least one same-day-8K trade in NVIDIA stock, and continued to chair the Select Committee on Strategic Competition with China.
