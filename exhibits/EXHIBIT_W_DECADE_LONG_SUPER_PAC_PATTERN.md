**Primary sources.** FEC committee profiles for Californians for Innovation (C00566679) and PROTECT PROGRESS (C00848440); FEC Schedule E independent-expenditure filings for the 2014, 2016, and 2024 cycles; FEC individual-contribution records; FEC operating-expenditure filings for Californians for Innovation 2014–2016.

**Pleading scope.** The 2014 and 2016 cycle conduct described in §§2–3 is presented as documentary record context the Commission may consider when evaluating the 2024 PROTECT PROGRESS coordination indicia under 11 C.F.R. § 109.21 and *FEC v. Cruz for Senate*, 596 U.S. 289 (2022). The 2024 cycle conduct described in §5 — together with §7's 2026 multi-jurisdictional continuation as it bears on the 2024 question — is the conduct pleaded as substantive basis for Count 4 of the accompanying Office of Congressional Conduct complaint.

## 1. Summary

Across his federal career, Representative Ro Khanna has been the beneficiary of three discrete waves of dedicated tech-industry-aligned Super PAC support, sustained over twelve years. The donor sub-industry evolved — from generalist Silicon Valley venture capital in 2014 and 2016 to cryptocurrency companies and executives in 2024 — and the structural role of the vehicle has been continuously filled across three contested or legislatively-active cycles: in each of those cycles, a Silicon-Valley-industry-aligned Super PAC underwrote Representative Khanna's campaign; in safe-seat cycles with no active industry signature legislation, no such vehicle operated.

Combined direct support and oppose-Honda independent expenditures across the two Khanna-supportive vehicles total **$1,369,595.92** ($1.37 million). Of that, $1,261,050.13 was deployed by Californians for Innovation across the 2014 and 2016 cycles and $108,545.79 by PROTECT PROGRESS in 2024. The 2026 cycle row reflects the deployment posture of the same upstream FAIRSHAKE / PROTECT PROGRESS compliance pipeline through April 29, 2026; its substantive content is set out at § 7.1 below and frames the post-2024 magnitude-and-direction shift the cryptocurrency-industry donor cohort effected at the federal superPAC layer.

## 2. Wave 1 — Californians for Innovation, 2014 cycle

Californians for Innovation (CFI), FEC committee C00566679, was registered as an Independent-Expenditure-Only Committee on May 9, 2013 for the stated purpose of supporting Representative Khanna's first run against incumbent Representative Mike Honda (D-CA-17). Across its operating life — the 2014 and 2016 cycles — every dollar of CFI's independent expenditures was directed to the Khanna–Honda race, and CFI supported no other federal candidate. Its treasurer was wealth manager Ash Chopra.

## 3. Opposition spending in 2014 — Working for Us PAC

For completeness of the record: in 2014 the labor coalition backing incumbent Representative Honda ran opposition spending against Representative Khanna through Working for Us PAC (FEC committee C00430876). This is the only independent expenditure against Representative Khanna's campaign across his House career on record. In 2014, Working for Us PAC spent $83,778 opposing Representative Khanna across two disbursements on May 28 and May 30, 2014. Working for Us PAC was funded by S. After Representative Khanna's 2016 victory, no PAC has run independent expenditures against him on record.

## 4. The industry-slot pattern — filled when contested or when industry legislation pends

Across Representative Khanna's career, the structural role of a dedicated Silicon-Valley-industry-aligned Super PAC has been filled in three cycles and unfilled in three:

| Cycle | Industry Super PAC supporting Rep. Khanna | Context |
|---|---|---|
| 2014 | Californians for Innovation ($391,287.59) | Open-seat run against labor-aligned incumbent; industry positioning Khanna as reformer |
| 2016 | Californians for Innovation ($177,089.77) | Rematch; same structural position |
| 2018 | — | Safe-seat incumbency; no industry signature legislation pending |
| 2020 | — | Safe-seat incumbency |
| 2022 | — | Safe-seat incumbency |
| 2024 | PROTECT PROGRESS ($108,545.79) | New legislative window: FIT21 + SAB 121 veto override; cryptocurrency-industry signature legislation |
| 2026 (through 2026-04-29) | — (zero support-IE for Khanna; FAIRSHAKE / PROTECT PROGRESS deployment redirected per § 7.1) | Safe-seat incumbency; pro-industry voting record continued through 2025 (CLARITY + GENIUS); industry priority redirected to opposition expenditures against Illinois Democratic primary candidates and to non-slate Member support |

The 2018–2022 absence is consistent with the pattern: when no industry signature legislation is pending and no contested election requires outside support, no dedicated Super PAC is activated. The 2024 reactivation occurred during the period when the cryptocurrency industry organized FIT21 and the SAB 121 veto override into priority federal legislation. The 2026-cycle pattern through April 29, 2026 differs from the 2018–2022 absence pattern in kind, not just in degree: the cryptocurrency-industry compliance pipeline did not stand down — it remained operationally active at $13.58M of opposition expenditures and $5.39M of redirected support — but routed zero of its 2026-cycle deployment to Representative Khanna or to any of the other sixteen House and two Senate beneficiaries the same pipeline backed in 2024.

The 2024 legislative window produced two roll-call dyads on which Representative Khanna's voting record was favorable to the cryptocurrency industry's position. On FIT21 (H.R. 4763) Representative Khanna voted YEA on May 22, 2024 (House Roll 226). On SAB 121 (H.J.Res. 109) Representative Khanna's position reversed within the same legislative window: he voted NAY on initial passage on May 8, 2024 (Roll 189) and then YEA on the veto override sixty-four days later on July 11, 2024 (Roll 355), on identical resolution text with no intervening textual amendment. The intra-2024 SAB 121 position-flip is paired with a contemporaneous donor-flow envelope — sustained pre-flip senior-executive contribution baseline; quiet sixty-four-day flip-window; new-CEO contribution surge initiating within twenty-five days of the override Yea — set out in detail at the accompanying Exhibit P §3 SAB 121 sub-sections. PROTECT PROGRESS's two 2024 independent expenditures supporting Representative Khanna landed approximately three months after the override Yea, consistent with post-flip sequencing as discussed in Exhibit P §3 and Exhibit J §4.

### 4.1 Peer-46 active-trader cohort cross-reference

Within the 46-Member active-trader peer cohort the complainant has compiled for comparative purposes across this filing, only two Members received any PROTECT PROGRESS independent-expenditure support during the 2024 cycle: Representative Josh Gottheimer (NJ-05) at approximately $120,103, and the respondent at $108,545.79. The remaining 44 of 46 peers in the cohort received zero PROTECT PROGRESS independent-expenditure support in 2024. The respondent's rank within that cohort is 2 of 46 (approximately the 97.8th percentile).

At the chamber-wide grain, PROTECT PROGRESS supported a total of twenty Democratic federal candidates across all 2024 races; within the 435-Member House universe, only a small minority were beneficiaries, and the chamber baseline likewise places the respondent in the upper tail. Both the peer cohort distribution and the chamber-wide baseline therefore agree in direction: receipt of PROTECT PROGRESS independent-expenditure support was an outlier event among Members of the House in 2024.

## 7. The 2026 state-and-federal expansion

The 2024 PROTECT PROGRESS window extended into a 2026 state-and-federal expansion. Across the first quarter of 2026 the same cryptocurrency-industry principals deployed at least $18.75 million across four California state-level political committees, operating through two structurally independent compliance stacks — one of them supervised by the same treasurer (Brandon Philipczyk) who serves PROTECT PROGRESS and FAIRSHAKE at the federal level. In parallel, FAIRSHAKE transferred an additional $4 million into PROTECT PROGRESS on February 9, 2026 (the committee's only 2026-cycle affiliate-transfer inflow on record). Mr. Larsen of Ripple Labs has separately deployed approximately $10.9 million in disclosed political contributions at the San Francisco city grain across 2019–2026. The cohort of donors, the temporal concentration, and the overlap in federal-compliance infrastructure are continuous with the federal PROTECT PROGRESS pipeline that Exhibit BB describes. The state-layer activity is documented in Exhibit DD.

The state-and-city mirror situates PROTECT PROGRESS as one engagement in a multi-jurisdictional compliance practice run by a common treasurer for a common cryptocurrency-industry donor cohort, operating continuously from the 2023 pre-cycle seed through Q1-2026.

### 7.1 The 2026-cycle federal-superPAC magnitude-and-direction shift

The same federal compliance pipeline at the heart of the 2024 PROTECT PROGRESS coordination indicia did not stand down at the 2024-to-2026 cycle boundary; it redeployed. Across the 2026 cycle through April 29, 2026, FAIRSHAKE PAC (C00835959) and PROTECT PROGRESS (C00848440) — the two committees with the shared treasurer of record, the shared registered street-and-suite address, and the documented $35.7 million 2023–2026 affiliate-transfer pipeline addressed at Exhibit BB — deployed independent expenditures of $18,971,009.41, of which **zero** flowed to Representative Khanna and **zero** flowed to any of the other sixteen House and two Senate Members the same pipeline supported in 2024. The 2026 deployment decomposes as follows:

- **FAIRSHAKE 2026 — pivot to opposition-only.** FAIRSHAKE's 2026-cycle Schedule E filings record 48 independent-expenditure transactions totaling **$13,582,415.23**, of which the entire $13,582,415.23 is `oppose` deployment and zero is `support` deployment whole-committee. The 2024-cycle FAIRSHAKE baseline was bidirectional — approximately $27.14 million supporting fourteen Democratic House and twelve Republican House candidates, and approximately $13.53 million opposing three Democratic primary challengers (Porter, Bowman, Bush). The 2026 pivot is therefore empirically a **magnitude-and-direction** shift rather than a withdrawal: opposition deployment retained at near-identical scale ($13.53 million → $13.58 million, +0.4 percent), while the bipartisan support arm was amputated entirely ($27.14 million → $0.00, −100 percent). The 2026 opposition is concentrated against three Democratic primary candidates in Illinois — Lieutenant Governor Juliana Stratton (S6IL00458, $10.24 million), Representative Robin Kelly (Ford H6IL07354, $2.53 million in IL-07), and Robert Peters (H6IL02298, $0.82 million in IL-02). All three opposition targets are Democratic primary candidates; none are Republicans. The 2024 cycle's "PAC supports Democrats and opposes anti-crypto Democrats" framing collapses into a narrower 2026 posture: oppose anti-crypto Democratic primary candidates while withdrawing affirmative deployment behind any of the cohort the same PAC backed two cycles earlier.

- **PROTECT PROGRESS 2026 — same magnitude, redirected cohort.** PROTECT PROGRESS retained substantive 2026-cycle support-IE deployment — 33 transactions totaling **$5,388,594.18** across nine candidates — but routed zero of that aggregate to Representative Khanna and zero to any of the other sixteen House and two Senate Members the same committee supported in 2024. The committee additionally received its sole 2026-cycle 18G affiliate-transfer inflow of $4,000,000 from FAIRSHAKE on February 9, 2026, consistent with continued operational integration of the upstream pipeline.

The 2026 federal-superPAC layer therefore exhibits a deployment posture of **continued operational activity at near-2024 magnitude paired with complete withdrawal of affirmative support for the 2024 beneficiary cohort.** The structural fact bears on the 2024 coordination indicia in two directions. First, it forecloses the anticipated defense framing that the 2024 PROTECT PROGRESS deployment reflected ordinary multi-cycle political support that "moves on once a Member becomes safer," because the same pipeline neither stood down nor diversified its cohort within the safe-seat — it dropped the cohort while continuing to deploy at scale. Second, it confirms the 2024 deployment's task-specific character: the PAC made specific independent-expenditure commitments coincident with cryptocurrency-industry signature legislation in the 118th Congress, and at the 119th Congress the PAC redeployed, having received the votes-of-record (FIT21, SAB 121 override, CLARITY, GENIUS) the deployment was directed toward.

The 2026-cycle data point is independent of, and parallel to, the state-staged GROW CALIFORNIA war chest's deployment dormancy through California's March 3, 2026 primary documented at Exhibit DD § 6, and parallel to the personal-retail-FEC-channel maintenance pattern at Exhibit BB § 5 (Larsen, Andreessen, and Horowitz each maintaining $7,000 cycle-cap individual contributions to Ro For Congress in Q1-2025 at the new 2025-2026 cycle boundary). The empirical channel-split — IE-coordinated channel withdrawn at the slate; personal-retail channel maintained at the per-principal cap — is part of the 2026 record. The Commission may evaluate the magnitude-and-direction shift alongside the 2024 coordination indicia rather than as a substitute for it.

## 8. The continuity finding

Representative Khanna's first House seat in 2016 followed Californians for Innovation's approximately $1.26 million combined support-Khanna and oppose-Honda spending across the 2014 and 2016 cycles. Representative Honda was a seven-term incumbent with deep labor ties, and the labor coalition's own independent spending against Representative Khanna in 2014 exceeded a million dollars. Representative Khanna ran as the Silicon Valley reformer-insurgent.

Structurally, the 12-year pattern reflects a continuous alignment between Silicon Valley industry and Representative Khanna's federal career. The particular industry sub-vertical evolves — generalist venture capital in 2014 and 2016; cryptocurrency in 2024 — and the structural role persists, filled in cycles when contested elections or signature legislation make outside industry support politically valuable.

The 2024 PROTECT PROGRESS coordination indicia at the heart of Count 4 of the accompanying Office of Congressional Conduct complaint sit within a 12-year industry-aligned Super PAC pattern, of which the 2024 cycle is the third installment.
