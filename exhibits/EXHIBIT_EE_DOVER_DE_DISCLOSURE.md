# Exhibit EE — Dover, Delaware Rental-Property Asymmetric Disclosure

The respondent's Annual Personal Financial Disclosures carry a ten-year structural asymmetry on one real-estate interest: a Dover, Delaware rental property whose **mortgage liability is disclosed once** on a single Annual Schedule D, while the **asset-side Schedule A entry is absent from every one of the ten Annual filings covering tax years 2014 through 2023**. Six other rental properties in the household's Schedule A inventory over the same ten-year window are correctly paired with asset and income entries. The Dover, Delaware rental is the sole asymmetric outlier. This exhibit sets out the ten-year disclosure table, the six paired-disclosure exemplars, and the two-portal Dover / Kent County DE property-records null result that together foreclose the reasonable-interpretation and good-faith-confusion defenses.

---

**One Schedule D liability entry across the ten-year window; zero Schedule A asset entries; zero Schedule B transactions; zero Schedule C earned-income lines.** The single disclosed liability stands without a companion asset across every adjacent filing year.

A rental property carrying a $100,001–$250,000 mortgage balance is economically certain to generate annual rental income in excess of the $200 disclosure threshold. Mortgage interest alone on a balance in that band at any post-2003 rate exceeds $200 per month; a property generating under $200 per year in rent would not support its own debt service. The rental-property structure implicit in the Schedule D entry ("Mortgage on Rental Property, Dover, DE") accordingly triggers the § 13104(a)(1)(B) unearned-income disclosure obligation independently of the § 13104(a)(3) real-property asset disclosure obligation.

The reasonable-interpretation and good-faith-confusion defenses are foreclosed on the face of the record.

## 3. Two-portal Dover / Kent County DE property-records null

Independent authoritative Dover / Kent County DE property-records databases were probed for any property titled in respondent's, the respondent's spouse's, or any Ahuja-family entity's name.

**Kent County DE Levy Court Property Records System** (pride.kentcountyde.gov, PRIDE 2021 system) owner-name property search returned zero matches for all of:

```
KHANNA · AHUJA · AHUJA REAL ESTATE · AHUJA FAMILY · ARECO ·
AHUJA RITU · KHANNA RITU · AHUJA REV · KHANNA REV
```

**City of Dover DE parcel database** (dover.ias-clt.com, operated by Tyler CLT, quarterly refresh) returned "Sorry! The search did not return any results" for each of:

```
KHANNA · AHUJA · RITU · KHANNA ROHIT · AHUJA RITU · ARECO
```

Both authoritative Dover / Kent County DE property-record databases accordingly show no property titled in respondent's, the respondent's spouse's, or any Ahuja-family LLC name. Three possible explanations:

- **(A) Nominee-name title.** The Dover, DE rental is titled in a third-party nominee name — a trust, LLC, or pass-through entity whose name does not match Ahuja, Khanna, or ARECO — that was not captured by the nine name probes above. If so, that nominee entity is itself an interest in real property held for investment or the production of income, categorically reportable under § 13104(a)(3), and its absence from Schedule A is independently a disclosure defect.
- **(B) Extraction artifact.** The "Dover, DE" description on the TY2021 Schedule D is a transcription artifact. This explanation is implausible because the lender identity — **First Bank of Wilmington, DE** — binds the state to Delaware on an independent field.
- **(C) Post-2021 sale.** The property was sold between the TY2021 Schedule D entry and the 2026 property-records lookup. This explanation is inconsistent with the disclosed "04/03" date_incurred (indicating an April 2003 acquisition) and with an active $100,001–$250,000 unpaid mortgage balance as of TY2021.

**AHUJA REAL ESTATE LLC** (Delaware Division of Corporations file #6008477) is the primary candidate for a nominee-name title-holder. The Delaware Division of Corporations entity-detail drill is gated by a server-side CAPTCHA, and the OpenCorporates mirror is likewise gated. The nominee-identity resolution is an open information-barrier question, not an exculpatory finding — the records-access constraint strengthens rather than weakens the structural-asymmetry finding.

## 4. Per-year violation structure

Civil penalties under 5 U.S.C. § 13106 attach per filing year. If the Committee on Ethics finds that each of tax years 2014 through 2023 failed to disclose an interest in real property (and the associated rental income above the $200 threshold) for the Dover, Delaware rental, the record supports **ten separate § 13106 violations**:

- $200 minimum per year after any annual waiver applied at Committee discretion;
- Theoretical ceiling per year under § 13106 of between $6,500 and $50,000 depending on knowing-and-willful findings and inflation-adjusted ranges;
- Combined exposure across the ten-year window between $65,000 (floor) and approximately $500,000 (maximum).

The respondent's affirmative-defense burden to rebut the structural asymmetry is narrow: either (a) documentary evidence that the property was rented below the $200 annual threshold across all ten years (implausible given the mortgage balance); (b) documentary evidence that the mortgage was the respondent's spouse's pre-marital separate obligation falling outside reporting and that the property was held below § 13104(a)(3) thresholds (inconsistent with the household's joint-disclosure practice on the six paired exemplars above); or (c) evidence of a nominee entity whose interest is itself disclosable on Schedule A and was also not disclosed. Each of the three rebuttal paths converges on a disclosure obligation that is absent from the ten-PFD record.

## 6. Methodology

The ten-year disclosure scan draws on a per-page re-extraction of the ten Annual PFDs (House Clerk docs listed in the Section 1 table) with the scan predicate:

```
match any row where:
  asset_name OR description OR creditor_name ILIKE '%Dover%'
                                           OR '%Delaware%'
                                           OR '%First Bank of Wilmington%';
  OR (schedule_letter = 'B' AND fields ILIKE '%rental%Dover%')
```

Result set: one matching row in 2,524 — the TY2021 doc 8219143 Schedule D liability. Zero Schedule A real-estate asset hits, zero Schedule B transaction hits, zero Schedule C earned-income hits across the ten-PFD window. The six paired-disclosure exemplars (Section 2) were identified by a second scan over the same 2,524 rows for Schedule A asset entries with `income_type = 'Rent'` paired with a populated `income_amount` band.

The Dover / Kent County DE two-portal property-records probe (Section 3) covered both the Kent County Levy Court PRIDE system and the City of Dover Tyler CLT parcel database with nine name variations across respondent, respondent's spouse, and three Ahuja-family entity name patterns.
