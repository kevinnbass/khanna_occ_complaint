# Exhibit J — Khanna × Gottheimer Coordination Audit

**Subjects.** Rep. Ro Khanna (CA-17) and Rep. Josh Gottheimer (NJ-5).

**Question.** Do donor, trading, and vote data establish Member-pair coordination that would extend the dark-money-ecosystem and donor-vote theories from a single-Member framing to a coordinated Member pair?

**Short answer.** Partially. Ten crypto-industry decision-maker donors max out to both Members at or near the $6,600 FECA two-year cycle cap; the two Members align on four of five signature 118th-Congress crypto-industry bills; but the bulk-donor-base overlap is below peer baseline, Gottheimer holds no crypto-exchange or mining equities, and same-day trade coordination concentrates in mainstream Big Tech and mainstream pharma rather than in industry-sensitive names. The coordination signal lives in the donor and vote axes, not the trading axis.

## 3. Shared-vendor disclosure test — Global Strategy Group

Under 11 C.F.R. § 109.21(d)(4), a pollster or strategic-research vendor who serves a candidate within 120 days of providing the same service to an Independent Expenditure committee spending on that candidate's behalf creates a rebuttable presumption of coordination. Global Strategy Group LLC (New York) is the common vendor on one side of the Member pair:

| Committee | Payments to Global Strategy Group | Transactions | Dates |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| PROTECT PROGRESS (FEC ID C00848440) | $132,500 | 3 | 2023-11-01; 2024-07-18; 2024-09-18 |
| Gottheimer principal committee (FEC ID C00573949) | $237,500 | 9 | 2019-10-03 through 2024-11-13 |

The 2024-07-18 and 2024-09-18 PROTECT PROGRESS polling payments sit within the 120-day coordination window of Gottheimer's 2024-08-07 and 2024-10-17 polling payments to the same firm. PROTECT PROGRESS separately spent an Independent-Expenditure amount benefiting Gottheimer's 2024 general-election campaign.

Respondent's side of the pair has no substantive common-vendor overlap with PROTECT PROGRESS. The only shared vendor on respondent's ledger is Amalgamated Bank ($12,378 in fees), which is the Democratic-institutional depository common to most Democratic committees — a fee-level relationship, not a strategic-research relationship. Treasurers are distinct (Khanna: David Donaldson; Gottheimer: Batya Klein; PROTECT PROGRESS: Brandon Philipczyk).

The § 109.21(d)(4) common-vendor coordination question accordingly attaches to Gottheimer through PROTECT PROGRESS via Global Strategy Group, not to respondent.

## 5. Disposition

- The donor-overlap signal is concentrated and max-out — the industry's decision-maker tier simultaneously at the $6,600 per-cycle cap to both Members — rather than broad.
- The crypto-bill-vote signal places both Members in the caucus top quartile, with one split on an early procedural vote and convergence on the override.
- The common-vendor test under 11 C.F.R. § 109.21(d)(4) attaches to Gottheimer through PROTECT PROGRESS via Global Strategy Group, not to respondent.
- The Member-pair trade-coordination signal is weak — shared trading concentrates in market-beta Big Tech and mainstream pharma, and Gottheimer holds none of the crypto-exchange, crypto-mining, or defense-prime tickers where industry-sensitive timing would be visible.

The pair is coordinated at the donor and vote level in a limited, industry-specific way, not at the trading level. The stronger independent case attaches to Gottheimer on the donor-and-vendor axis; respondent's side of the pair is addressed in this filing package on the donor, vote, and Independent-Expenditure axes as a single-Member matter.
