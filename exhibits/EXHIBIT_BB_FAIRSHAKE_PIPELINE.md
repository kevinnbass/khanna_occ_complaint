# Exhibit BB — The FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS pipeline: shared treasurer, shared address, shared administrative infrastructure

**Primary sources.** FEC committee profiles for FAIRSHAKE (C00835959) and PROTECT PROGRESS (C00848440) at https://www.fec.gov/data/; FEC bulk committee master tables for the 2022 and 2024 cycles (`lake.fec_committee_master_2022`, `lake.fec_committee_master_2024`); FEC committee-to-committee transfer records for the 2024 and 2026 cycles (`lake.fec_committee_transfers_2024`, `_2026`); FEC Schedule E filings for the 2024 cycle; Senate Lobbying Disclosure Act filings with the House and Senate Clerks.

## 1. Summary

The Super PAC that spent independent-expenditure dollars supporting Representative Khanna's 2024 re-election (PROTECT PROGRESS, C00848440) and the cryptocurrency industry's flagship Super PAC (FAIRSHAKE, C00835959) are not arm's-length committees that happened to do business together. They share the same treasurer of record and the same administrative infrastructure; throughout the 2024-cycle conduct period at issue both committees shared the identical registered street-and-suite address (2740 SW Martin Downs Blvd #51, Palm City, FL 34990) on each committee's active Form 1 Statement of Organization, and on July 7, 2025 both committees filed simultaneous amended Form 1 statements moving to a shared new address at a second commercial mail-receiving agency (2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116, also a UPS Store retail location offering private mailbox rental services). FAIRSHAKE funneled $35.7 million into PROTECT PROGRESS in a single sustained pipeline during 2023 through 2026. On the single day PROTECT PROGRESS paid its largest single disbursement supporting Representative Khanna, FAIRSHAKE wired PROTECT PROGRESS $2 million. Parallel to this committee-level pipeline, the same cryptocurrency donors lobbied every one of the seven federal financial regulators that would administer the crypto-asset legislation on which Representative Khanna voted.

Exhibit G sets out the PROTECT PROGRESS-to-Khanna leg of the pipeline. Exhibit P situates Representative Khanna within PROTECT PROGRESS's twenty-candidate 2024 slate. This exhibit sets out the upstream FAIRSHAKE-to-PROTECT-PROGRESS leg, the shared administrative infrastructure that links the two committees, the same-day funds-tracing concurrence, and the parallel agency-lobbying footprint. Together the three exhibits describe a single coordinated financial and advocacy structure operating on the candidate Representative Khanna's behalf.

Five FEC-registered Super PACs — including both the crypto-industry flagship FAIRSHAKE and the Khanna-beneficiary PROTECT PROGRESS — thus designate the same Palm City, Florida street address on each committee's active Form 1 Statement of Organization as of the 2024-cycle conduct period, with a single person, Brandon Philipczyk, signing as treasurer for each. FAIRSHAKE and PROTECT PROGRESS share the identical suite designator (#51); MAJORITY MAKERS PAC and WEGIVEBLUE share the identical suite designator (#41); UNITED TOMORROW carries no suite designator. The two-committee co-occurrence at suite #51 places the cryptocurrency-industry flagship Super PAC and the Khanna-beneficiary Super PAC at the same registered mail point under the same treasurer of record.

The street address itself is a commercial mail-receiving agency — The UPS Store at 2740 SW Martin Downs Boulevard, Palm City — that offers private mailbox rental services. The "#51" and "#41" suite designators carried on the Form 1 Statements of Organization are private mailbox numbers issued by that retail mail-receiving establishment, not separate office suites in a multi-tenant office building. The structural significance of the shared registered address is therefore that five FEC-registered Super PACs and the same statutory-principal treasurer route their official Commission correspondence through one commercial retail mail-receiving establishment, with FAIRSHAKE and PROTECT PROGRESS routing through the same private mailbox at that establishment. The Form 1 record does not identify a physically distinct compliance-services firm at that address; the named statutory principal at each committee is Mr. Philipczyk himself, whose 2024-cycle treasurer portfolio is set out at § 3 below.

### 2a. The July 7, 2025 simultaneous amendments to a second commercial mail-receiving agency

On July 7, 2025 — the same calendar day — both FAIRSHAKE (C00835959) and PROTECT PROGRESS (C00848440) filed amended Form 1 Statements of Organization moving the registered address of each committee from 2740 SW Martin Downs Blvd #51, Palm City, FL 34990 to a shared new address at 2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116. The remaining three Palm City cluster committees (MAJORITY MAKERS PAC, WEGIVEBLUE, UNITED TOMORROW) retained the Palm City registration and were not party to the July 7, 2025 amendment cycle. The 2024-cycle baseline analysis in this exhibit is anchored to the cluster composition as of the conduct period.

The new shared registered address — 2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116 — is also a commercial mail-receiving agency: The UPS Store, location #6160, which advertises private mailbox rental services with a real street address designated by either the "PMB" prefix or the pound symbol ("#"), package acceptance from all carriers, mail receipt notifications, and mail holding and forwarding services on the same retail-MRA model as the Palm City UPS Store. The structural posture of the FAIRSHAKE/PROTECT PROGRESS shared registered address therefore did not change in character on July 7, 2025: both committees continued to share a single private-mailbox location at a commercial UPS Store retail establishment, under the same treasurer of record, after the amendment as before it.

The July 7, 2025 same-calendar-day Form 1 amendment co-occurrence is itself probative of shared administrative infrastructure under the affiliated-committee analytic framework at 11 C.F.R. § 100.5(g) and § 102.6: simultaneous filing of identical-target address amendments by two committees with a shared statutory-principal treasurer is the operational signature of a single compliance practice administering both committees as engagements of the same vendor. The amendment cycle accordingly strengthens, rather than weakens, the §§ 2-3 shared-administrative-infrastructure record.

The Federal Election Campaign Act makes the treasurer the statutory principal of any committee: 52 U.S.C. §30102(a) provides that the committee treasurer is legally responsible for keeping records and filing reports, and 11 C.F.R. §102.7(a) requires the treasurer to authorize and supervise the committee's expenditures. A shared treasurer is therefore not the arm's-length overlap of two independent operations; it is a shared statutory principal. Under the coordinated-communications framework at 11 C.F.R. §109.20, shared vendors and shared consultants trigger a coordination analysis; a shared treasurer at a shared address is the stronger form of that indicium, because the same individual is legally responsible for both the transferring committee's spending decisions and the receiving committee's spending decisions.

### 3a. Within-cluster affiliate-transfer pipeline concentration

The 2024-cycle ranking of all 1,970 same-(treasurer, address, ZIP) Super-PAC + Hybrid-PAC clusters by within-cluster `18G` affiliate-transfer total identifies only two clusters above ten million dollars: rank #1 is Rebecca Lambe at 1032 15th Street NW, Washington DC ($312.9 million across 39 transactions, the Senate Majority PAC and House Majority PAC apparatus integral to the Democratic Party's national independent-expenditure infrastructure), and rank #2 is the Palm City Philipczyk cluster ($31.7 million across seven transactions, all flowing FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS, with zero within-cluster transfers to UNITED TOMORROW, MAJORITY MAKERS, or WEGIVEBLUE).

That single-sector-sink concentration of $31.7 million through one within-cluster pipeline to PROTECT PROGRESS — the committee that then made the Khanna-supporting October 4, 2024 disbursement on the same business day FAIRSHAKE wired in $2 million — is the structural attribute that distinguishes the Palm City pipeline. Two analogues in the broader chamber-wide population — the Lambe SMP/HMP party-adjacent apparatus, and the Palm City single-industry pipeline — exhaust the population of clusters with that magnitude of within-cluster 18G concentration.

## 4. The FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS transfer pipeline — $35.7 million

Across the 2023 pre-cycle period, the 2024 election cycle, and the 2026 cycle, FAIRSHAKE transferred **$35,700,000** into PROTECT PROGRESS across eight committee-to-committee transfers, each of them filed as FEC transaction type `18G` (Transfer From Affiliates/Other):

This pipeline is PROTECT PROGRESS's single largest inflow source across the entire 2023–2026 period. Committee-to-committee transfers between affiliated Super PACs are not subject to the individual or PAC contribution caps at 52 U.S.C. §30116, so the $35.7 million moved cleanly under §30116's affiliate-transfer exception. Whether the two committees are, for independence-of-expenditure purposes, distinct is a separate question from whether the upstream transfers themselves were lawful.

## 5. The same-day concurrence — October 4, 2024

On October 4, 2024 two events in the FEC record coincide precisely:

- FAIRSHAKE transferred **$2,000,000** to PROTECT PROGRESS (FAIRSHAKE 18G transfer #7 in §4).
- PROTECT PROGRESS disbursed **$100,000** to Targeted Platform Media LLC for a Khanna-supporting media buy (PROTECT PROGRESS Schedule E; see Exhibit G §3).

Under federal funds-tracing doctrine, a same-day receipt and disbursement pattern supports the inference that the receiving committee was funded to spend on the specific disbursement — that the particular dollars paid to the media vendor for the Khanna communication moved, within a single business day, through PROTECT PROGRESS from FAIRSHAKE's industry-donor balance sheet. The general two-hop pathway from crypto-industry donor to Khanna-supporting advertising tightens to a same-day concurrence at the October 4, 2024 node. That concurrence strengthens the source-attribution inference beyond the aggregate annual transfer figure.

### 5a. Quantification against the Khanna-supporting Schedule E ledger

The Khanna-supporting Schedule E ledger filed by PROTECT PROGRESS for the 2024 cycle consists, after deduplication of duplicate F24 forty-eight-hour-notice and F3X quarterly-report filings on each Federal Election Commission `transaction_id`, of two distinct disbursements: the $100,000 payment to Targeted Platform Media LLC dated October 4, 2024 (the media buy described in § 5 above), and an $8,545.79 payment to Backstory Strategies LLC dated October 21, 2024 (consulting services). Those two transactions are the entire universe of PROTECT PROGRESS independent expenditures in support of Representative Khanna for the cycle.

Tabulating the two Khanna-supporting Schedule E disbursement dates against the eight FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS § 18G transfer dates set out at § 4 yields:

| Pairing dimension | Count | Detail |
|---|---:|---|
| Same-day matches | 1 | October 4, 2024 — transfer #7 ($2,000,000) coincides with the Targeted Platform Media disbursement ($100,000) |
| Within-3-day matches | 1 | The same single observation; no second pairing within ±3 days |
| Within-7-day matches | 1 | The same single observation; the October 21 IE disbursement falls 17 days from the closest § 18G transfer date |

Under uniform random scheduling of two Khanna-supporting independent-expenditure dates across the 900-day window spanning the first (August 25, 2023) and last (February 9, 2026) FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS § 18G transfer, the expected number of same-day independent-expenditure-and-transfer matches is 8 × 2 / 900 ≈ 0.018, and the probability that the two-IE schedule yields at least one same-day hit on any of the eight § 18G transfer days is approximately 1.8 percent. The observed single same-day match falls at approximately fifty-six times the uniform-random expected count of matches.

The empirical Schedule E record therefore contains a single same-day concurrence — the October 4, 2024 alignment described in § 5 — rather than a repeating multi-event pattern of same-day concurrences across the eight § 18G transfer dates. The inferential weight of the single concurrence rests on its position relative to the uniform-random baseline: among the pairings the Khanna-supporting Schedule E ledger could have produced under independent scheduling, the observed October 4, 2024 alignment with FAIRSHAKE's seventh § 18G transfer to PROTECT PROGRESS is not a frequent expected outcome.

## 6. Parallel regulator-level advocacy — FIT21 lobbied at every federal financial regulator

The committee-level FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS pipeline runs parallel to a regulator-level advocacy campaign on the same cryptocurrency-industry legislation. Lobbying Disclosure Act filings for the 2023, 2024, and 2025 years that referenced H.R. 4763 ("FIT21") name **every one of the seven federal financial regulators** that would administer the bill's crypto-asset oversight provisions:

|---|---:|
| Treasury, Department of | 137 |
| Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) | 68 |
| Federal Reserve System | 58 |
| Internal Revenue Service (IRS) | 52 |
| Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) | 49 |
| Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) | 37 |
| Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) | 37 |

The three largest FAIRSHAKE-to-PROTECT-PROGRESS source donors — Coinbase, Ripple, and Andreessen Horowitz — appear on the FIT21 LDA filings at multiple agencies in parallel. Coinbase's own LDA filings on FIT21 name Treasury plus seven additional agencies; Anchor Labs (Coinbase Custody parent) names Treasury, SEC, the Federal Reserve, the IRS, and two further agencies. The Securities Industry and Financial Markets Association (SIFMA) lobbied FIT21 at all seven financial regulators, for a cumulative 114 regulator-level filing touches. The Digital Chamber, the Crypto Council for Innovation, the DeFi Education Fund, the Stellar Development Foundation, Nasdaq, the Blockchain Association, the Filecoin Foundation, and Bank of America Corp. round out a multi-regulator crypto-industry coalition.

The record shows two parallel advocacy tracks run by the same industry donors who fund both ends of the FAIRSHAKE-to-PROTECT-PROGRESS committee pipeline: (a) one track spending $35.7 million through an affiliate-transfer structure that reaches Representative Khanna's media placements; (b) a second track lobbying every federal agency that would administer the underlying legislation.

## 7. The pipeline as coordination-indicia evidence

The record documents:

- The two committees at the origin and endpoint of the crypto-industry-to-Khanna money flow share the same treasurer, the same address, and administrative infrastructure (§§ 2–3).
- The pipeline moved $35.7 million across eight transfers in a pattern consistent across two election cycles (§ 4).
- On the single day of PROTECT PROGRESS's largest Khanna-supporting disbursement, FAIRSHAKE wired PROTECT PROGRESS $2 million (§ 5).
- The same crypto-industry donors who supply this committee pipeline lobbied every federal financial regulator that would administer the underlying legislation (§ 6).

The pipeline is pleaded as coordination-indicia evidence under *McConnell v. FEC*, 540 U.S. 93 (2003), and 11 C.F.R. §109.21(d). Whether PROTECT PROGRESS's Khanna-supporting disbursements were independent of Representative Khanna's campaign — including the contemporaneous communications between Representative Khanna's campaign and either PROTECT PROGRESS or FAIRSHAKE — lies within the Federal Election Commission's subpoena authority under 52 U.S.C. §30107(a)(3), to be developed through the Commission's statutory investigative authority.
