# Exhibit T — Public Reform Statements vs. Household Trading Record

**Count supported.** Credibility framing for Count 1 and the §VI anticipated-response cluster on the separately-managed-account defense. Not a standalone STOCK Act §3, §4, or 5 U.S.C. § 13105(l) charging theory.

**Question.** On at least eight dated occasions during 2023–2026, respondent publicly endorsed banning Congressional stock trading, introduced reform legislation to that effect, and stated that he does not trade and has no input in his spouse's trust. What did the household trade in the ±30-day window around each of those statements?

---

## 1. Scope and method

This exhibit pairs eight dated public statements in which respondent endorsed a Congressional stock-trading ban (or co-sponsored / introduced reform legislation) with household Periodic Transaction Report activity within ±30 days of the statement date. Statements are sourced from Washington Examiner press accounts, X post timestamps, respondent's own legislative filings, and Newsweek reporting. Household trades are queried from the House Clerk PTR substrate filtered to the filer's household (member, spouse, dependent-child accounts).

The pairing is not a material-non-public-information claim. It is a juxtaposition of dated reform rhetoric with dated household trading volume in the surrounding window.

**Two-universe baseline.** Respondent's late-filing rate under 5 U.S.C. § 13105(l) is 2.84%, below the chamber median of 10.34% and below the peer-46 cohort median. Respondent's worst single late filing is 358 days, ranking chamber P49.5 (below median) and peer-46 P32.6 (below median). The probative weight of this exhibit is therefore not a rate-based or severity-based outlier framing; it is the gross volume of household trading concurrent with respondent's own anti-trading rhetoric.

## 6. Disposition

This exhibit supplies the credibility axis for the §VI anticipated-response cluster on the separately-managed-account defense. Respondent has been on record on at least eight dated occasions across 2023–2026 calling for a Congressional stock-trading ban that, by the terms of the TRUST in Congress Act he co-sponsors and H. Res. 938 he introduced, would apply to the Member, the spouse, and dependent children. The household placed thousands of trades within ±30 days of those statements at a midpoint disclosed dollar volume of approximately $93 million. The juxtaposition does not promote to a STOCK Act §3, §4, or 5 U.S.C. § 13105(l) violation; it stands as a contemporaneous rhetorical-versus-conduct record relevant to the separately-managed-account framing addressed elsewhere in the package.
