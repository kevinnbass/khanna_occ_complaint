## 1. Summary

Representative Khanna serves on the House Armed Services Committee in the 115th through 119th Congresses. His household — spouse Ritu Ahuja Khanna and dependent-child accounts — executed **15 defense-prime common-stock transactions on four NDAA enactment timelines** between November 2017 and December 2023. On each of the four windows, the household acquired or disposed of NDAA-beneficiary issuers within 14 days of enactment, and on three of the four the transactions clustered on a single trading day before enactment.

Representative Khanna **voted NAY on 12 of 13 NDAA passage and override motions** across that same period while the household accumulated defense-prime positions on the same bills' enactment calendars. The **public-NAY / private-BUY divergence** is the materiality fact underlying Count 1's committee-duty-to-trading nexus.

This exhibit lists every enactment-window transaction, the corresponding roll-call vote, and the peer-cohort baseline that places the 29-trade absolute-count record at the top of the House active-trader universe.

## 6. Aggregate pattern characteristics

- 15 defense-prime trades across four NDAA enactment windows, averaging 3.75 trades per window.
- Every window is within 14 days of enactment; the trades fall at −14, −12, −4, −13, and −2 days. No windowed trade lands on the post-enactment side of the signing.
- Every window exhibits same-day multi-ticker or multi-account clustering: seven primes in Window 1, four primes in Window 2, two-account dual-buy in Window 4.
- Zero post-enactment trades within the ±14-day window. The buying concentrates on the pre-enactment side, where any information asymmetry about appropriations magnitudes or program authorizations would produce the greater expected return.

## 8. Public-NAY / private-BUY divergence

Across the 115th through 119th Congresses, Representative Khanna cast **13 recorded roll-call votes on NDAA passage or veto-override motions** (one per Congress per authorization cycle, with two motions in the FY2021 override). Of those 13, **12 were NAY**. During the same period, the household accumulated defense-prime stock on four of those bills' enactment windows as documented in §§2–5.

The divergence is material to 15 U.S.C. § 78u-1(g) analysis. A Member who publicly opposes a defense-authorization measure — communicating to constituents a policy judgment that the underlying appropriations are objectionable — while the Member's household acquires stock in the measure's beneficiaries within days of enactment presents a record at variance with arm's-length, non-MNPI-driven portfolio management. The vote record does not cancel the benefit. The bills passed; the household positions appreciated; the NAY vote was a public disclosure the trade record contradicts.

The divergence implicates House Rule XXIII clause 1 (conduct reflecting creditably on the House) independently of whether MNPI is ultimately proved.

## 10. Statutory framing

- **15 U.S.C. § 78u-1(g)** (STOCK Act §§ 3–4): Rule 10b-5 is extended to Members of Congress who trade on material non-public information acquired in connection with legislative duty. Enactment-window trading on bill beneficiaries is the fact pattern the statute addresses.
- **House Rule XXIII clause 1:** conduct reflecting creditably on the House. The public-vote / private-trade divergence implicates this rule independently of an MNPI finding.
- **5 U.S.C. § 13106:** late-filing penalties apply to any of these 15 transactions whose PTR was filed more than 45 days after the transaction date. The per-transaction filing-date audit is in Exhibit LATE_FILING_AUDIT.
