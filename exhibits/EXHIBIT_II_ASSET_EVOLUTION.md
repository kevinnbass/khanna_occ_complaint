# Exhibit II — Household Asset Evolution Timeline and Family-Entity Inventory (TY2013 through TY2024)

This exhibit consolidates the per-year Annual Personal Financial Disclosure Schedule A record of respondent's household portfolio across tax years 2014 through 2024 (19 Annual and Amendment PFDs) and pairs that timeline with an inventory of the family-entity wrappers through which the household's disclosed assets are held. It is offered as reference substrate for Counts 3 and 7 and is read alongside Exhibit K (Khanna household Schedule A export, 223 rows) and Exhibit L (TY2024 Annual PFD full-document OCR).

The inventory picks up two classes of holding entity beyond the Ahuja Charitable Foundation developed at Exhibit H: (i) three spouse-settlor trusts (the 1994, 1995, and 1998 Ritu Ahuja Trusts; separately the Rita / Ritu Ahuja Declaration of Trust and its variants); and (ii) a set of generation-skipping and dependent-child-beneficiary trusts (the 2010 Grandchildren Trust with Monte and Usha Ahuja as settlors; the 2020 Trust FBO Khanna Children; the Ahuja Grandchildren's Education Trust). MURA Holdings LLC appears as a family-holding wrapper used across multiple generations' trust structures (including "Ritu Ahuja 1994 Trust: MURA Holdings: MAI Capital Management" and "Ritu Ahuja 1994 Trust: MURA Holdings: Cleveland Convelo Holdings LLC" nested-wrapper entries). M&R Trust Partnership is the Ahuja family limited-partnership vehicle.

Three implications follow for Counts 3 and 7:

- **(A) The Ahuja Foundation is embedded in a broader family-entity stack.** The Foundation (developed at Exhibit H) is one of at least fourteen distinct family-entity wrappers through which the household's Schedule A holdings flow. The spouse-as-trustee § 13104(d)(1)(A) disclosure-scope question at Count 7 does not turn on the Foundation alone; the same question is raised by the spouse's trustee relationship to the Ritu Ahuja Trust vehicles and to M&R Trust Partnership.
- **(B) Dependent-child and grandchildren-beneficiary trusts are active beneficial-ownership categories.** The 2020 Trust FBO Khanna Children (aggregate high $201M across 2021–2024) and the 2010 Grandchildren Trust (aggregate high $118M across 2021–2024) sit on the dependent-child side of the Schedule A owner-code column. The Count 7 disclosure-integrity question on dependent-child beneficial ownership is anchored on these vehicles in addition to Schedule A trading activity.
- **(C) Nested-wrapper entries obscure ultimate beneficial ownership.** Schedule A rows that identify a position as held through "Ritu Ahuja 1994 Trust: MURA Holdings, LLC: Cleveland Convelo Holdings LLC" describe a three-layer holding structure in a single asset-name field. The line is filed as a single Schedule A row, not as a separate Schedule A entry for each nested entity, which defeats the asset-name-column index used by the House Clerk portal's disclosure-search interface. The same pattern recurs across "Ritu Ahuja 1994 Trust: MURA Holdings: MAI Capital Management" and "Ritu Ahuja 1994 Trust: MURA Holdings: Huntington Bank Checking" entries.
