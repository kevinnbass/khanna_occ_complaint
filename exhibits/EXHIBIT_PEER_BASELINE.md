---

## 1. Two-universe rule (read this first)

Every comparative claim in this exhibit is tested in **both** universes:

- **Active-trader peer cohort (n=46)** — the subset of House Members who file many PTRs. This is the *harder* test: the subject competes against the chamber's most-active traders rather than against the median Member who barely trades.
- **Chamber-wide (n=210–780 depending on metric)** — all House Members with sufficient substrate coverage to produce a stable percentile.

Where the two universes agree on direction, both percentiles are cited. Where they diverge, the divergence is disclosed in the metric's own section, not hidden. No metric in this exhibit is cited out of only the universe that favors the complaint.

## 7. Methodology, caveats, and reproducibility

### Cohort construction

The 46-Member active-trader cohort is defined as:

- 10 explicit Tier-1 subjects: the subject, Pelosi, Crenshaw, Gottheimer, Greene, Mast, Huizenga, Fallon, Harshbarger, Hern.
- 40 top-PTR-filers in `lake.house_ptr_index` (2017–2026), deduplicated against the Tier-1 list.
- 6 HASC-overlap Members surfaced by both criteria (the subject, Fallon, Conaway, Lamborn, Green TN, Brooks).

The cohort is intentionally biased toward high-volume traders; the full-chamber denominator (via `public.house_ptr_chamber_audit_dollar_weighted`, n=210 with ≥20 tx) is the corroborating universe.

### Amendment-cascade attribution

All late-rate and worst-delay metrics route through `lake.house_ptr_transactions_canonical`, which dedupes amendment re-reports by `(filer, asset, tx_date, tx_type, owner)` and attributes `days_late` to the *earliest* filing that disclosed each transaction. Raw-join numbers (which double-count amendments as fresh late filings) are no longer operative.

### Pre-swearing-in filter

For the subject, pre-2017-01-03 transactions are excluded; the 45-day STOCK Act deadline applies only to transactions occurring during congressional tenure. The chamber audit applies the same filter per-Member via a LATERAL join to `lake.congress_members.served_from`.

### PFD-extraction coverage caveat

The PFD-derived metrics (M11–M16) depend on the V18+V9 extractor pipeline having run on each peer Member's PFD cycles. Peer-cohort coverage is high (43–46 of 46 peers populated per metric); chamber-wide coverage is lower (most non-cohort Members have thin extraction), which is why the peer-cohort percentile is the load-bearing figure and the chamber percentile appears only for directional corroboration where coverage permits.

### Ticker-coverage fallback

For metrics keying on `asset_ticker` (the defense-prime share M5 and NDAA-window M6), peer coverage includes an `asset_name ILIKE` fallback through the case-schema ticker map. Substantial ticker NULL rates across peer rows (and approximately 50% of Khanna's own PFD rows) do not affect the direction of M5 (P48 at median); M6 absolute counts are robust to ticker imputation (defense-prime tickers are lexically stable). The methodology disclosure in each metric's fact body records the NULL rate.

### Reproducibility

```sql
-- Peer percentile slate
SELECT metric_name, khanna_value, p25, p50, p75, p95
FROM ro_khanna.peer_baseline_percentiles
ORDER BY metric_name;

-- Full 46-row peer table
SELECT * FROM ro_khanna.peer_baseline ORDER BY total_filings DESC;

-- Chamber rate + worst + composite percentiles for the subject
WITH q AS (
  SELECT member_last_name, pct_late, worst_days_late, composite_score,
         PERCENT_RANK() OVER (ORDER BY pct_late) AS r_pct,
         PERCENT_RANK() OVER (ORDER BY worst_days_late) AS r_worst,
         PERCENT_RANK() OVER (ORDER BY composite_score DESC) AS r_comp
  FROM public.house_ptr_chamber_audit_dollar_weighted
  WHERE n_tx_total >= 20
)
SELECT * FROM q WHERE member_last_name = 'Khanna';
```
