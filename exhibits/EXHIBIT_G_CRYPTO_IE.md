# Exhibit G — Cryptocurrency-industry independent-expenditure support for Representative Khanna's 2024 re-election

**Primary sources.** FEC committee profiles C00848440 (PROTECT PROGRESS) and C00503185 (Ro For Congress) at https://www.fec.gov/data/; FEC Schedule E filings for the 2024 cycle; FEC individual-contribution records; House roll call 226 (H.R. 4763) of May 22, 2024.

---

## 1. Summary

In the final weeks of the 2024 general election, a single-industry Super PAC funded by cryptocurrency companies and crypto-industry venture capitalists made independent expenditures supporting Representative Rohit Khanna's re-election. The same principals who financed that Super PAC also made direct itemized contributions at or near the federal individual maximum to Representative Khanna's principal campaign committee. Five months before the independent expenditures, Representative Khanna voted for the cryptocurrency industry's flagship legislation. Four strands of the public record — the Super PAC's funding, its expenditures, the overlapping donor maxings, and the subsequent official act — present a pattern the Federal Election Commission is positioned to examine under the coordination doctrine articulated in *Citizens United v. FEC*.

## 2. The Super PAC: PROTECT PROGRESS

PROTECT PROGRESS is an Independent-Expenditure-Only Committee registered with the FEC on August 18, 2023 under committee ID **C00848440**, with treasurer Brandon Philipczyk. At the time of the 2024-cycle independent expenditures at issue the committee's Form 1 Statement of Organization recorded a registered street-and-suite address of 2740 SW Martin Downs Blvd #51, Palm City, FL 34990; on July 7, 2025 the committee filed an amended Form 1 moving to 2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116, the same calendar day FAIRSHAKE (C00835959) filed an analogous amended Form 1 to the same St. Paul address.

Through the 2023–2024 cycle the committee raised **$6,775,250 from twelve deduplicated donors**, each of which is a cryptocurrency-industry company or a principal of a crypto-industry venture firm:

| Donor | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Coinbase, Inc. | $2,500,000 |
| Ripple Labs, Inc. | $1,500,000 |
| Ronald Conway (SV Angel) | $1,000,000 |
| Marc Andreessen (Andreessen Horowitz) | $875,000 |
| Ben Horowitz (Andreessen Horowitz) | $875,000 |
| Multicoin Capital | $25,000 |
| Individual contributors (incl. Tushar Jain) | ~$250 |

No donor in the deduplicated funder set is unconnected to cryptocurrency-industry interests.

## 3. The independent expenditures supporting Representative Khanna

PROTECT PROGRESS made **two independent expenditures totalling $108,545.79 in support of Representative Khanna's 2024 re-election**, both in the final weeks of the general election:

- **October 4, 2024 — $100,000.00** paid to Targeted Platform Media LLC for a Khanna media buy.
- **October 21, 2024 — $8,545.79** paid to Backstory Strategies LLC for Khanna media production.

Each disbursement is recorded under a single transaction_id in the FEC Schedule E records (V01468454404BCE2C3D8 and V628C45040F06910ADED respectively). None of the committee's Schedule E disbursements opposed any candidate.

Representative Khanna was one of twenty federal Democratic candidates PROTECT PROGRESS supported in 2024; the full slate and its spending pattern are set out in Exhibit P. Across the curated 46-Member peer cohort against which Representative Khanna's trading and fundraising metrics are benchmarked, forty-four Members received $0 of PROTECT PROGRESS support; Representative Joshua Gottheimer (NJ-05) received $120,103 in PROTECT PROGRESS independent expenditures and is the only peer-cohort Member ranking ahead of Representative Khanna on this metric.

## 4. The overlapping direct donations to Representative Khanna's campaign committee

The same individuals who furnished the Super PAC's funds also made direct, itemized contributions to Representative Khanna's principal campaign committee, **Ro For Congress** (C00503185), at or near the federal individual maximum. Four PROTECT PROGRESS principals in particular:

- **Marc Andreessen** — ten direct contributions to Ro For Congress across 2015–2024.
- **Ben Horowitz** — $2,900 direct on May 24, 2022; his spouse Felicia Horowitz and family members Jaime Horowitz and Eliot Horowitz each contributed $3,300 on February 28, 2024.
- **Ronald Conway** — twenty-six direct contributions to Ro For Congress across 2013–2024; $3,300 maximum on February 15, 2023; his spouse Gayle Conway on a parallel schedule.
- **Tushar Jain** (Multicoin Capital) — $3,300 on August 10, 2024.

Taken together the PROTECT PROGRESS donor universe made 48 itemized direct contributions to Ro For Congress over 2013–2024 — the highest donor-universe overlap among the twenty-candidate beneficiary slate.

## 5. The subsequent vote on FIT21

Five months before the October 2024 independent expenditures, on May 22, 2024, Representative Khanna voted **YEA** on **H.R. 4763**, the Financial Innovation and Technology for the 21st Century Act ("FIT21"), House roll call 226. FIT21 was the cryptocurrency industry's central legislative priority of the 118th Congress: it moves primary jurisdiction over digital-commodity trading from the Securities and Exchange Commission to the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and removes SEC oversight from a defined set of digital-asset tokens. Coinbase, Ripple Labs, and Andreessen Horowitz — the three largest PROTECT PROGRESS funders — each lobbied for passage on the public record. The bill cleared the House 279–136, with seventy-one Democrats joining Republicans; Representative Khanna was among them.

## 6. The upstream funding structure — FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS

PROTECT PROGRESS is not a standalone single-industry Super PAC. Across the 2023–2026 period it received $35,700,000 from FAIRSHAKE (FEC committee C00835959), the cryptocurrency industry's flagship federal Super PAC, through eight committee-to-committee transfers. FAIRSHAKE and PROTECT PROGRESS share the same treasurer of record (Brandon Philipczyk), at the time of the 2024-cycle conduct at issue shared the identical registered street-and-suite address (2740 SW Martin Downs Blvd #51, Palm City, FL 34990) on each committee's active Form 1 Statement of Organization, subsequently filed simultaneous amended Form 1 statements on July 7, 2025 moving to a shared address at 2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116 (the same-calendar-day Form 1 amendment co-occurrence is itself additional shared-administrative-infrastructure evidence under the affiliated-committee analytic framework at 11 C.F.R. § 100.5(g) and § 102.6), and at the time of the 2024-cycle conduct at issue maintained a common administrative infrastructure that encompassed five federally registered Super PACs at the Palm City address. On October 4, 2024, FAIRSHAKE wired PROTECT PROGRESS $2,000,000 on the same day PROTECT PROGRESS paid $100,000 to Targeted Platform Media LLC for the Khanna-supporting media buy.

The Palm City structure is distinguished from the federal-Super-PAC ecosystem at large by within-cluster affiliate-transfer concentration. Among 1,970 distinct (treasurer, street-and-suite, ZIP) Super-PAC clusters on the 2024 cycle, only two clusters carry within-cluster `18G` transfer aggregates above ten million dollars: Rebecca Lambe's Senate Majority PAC / House Majority PAC apparatus at 1032 15th Street NW Washington DC ($312,900,000, the Democratic Party's national independent-expenditure infrastructure), and the Palm City Philipczyk cluster ($31,700,000, all flowing FAIRSHAKE → PROTECT PROGRESS, with zero within-cluster transfers to UNITED TOMORROW, MAJORITY MAKERS PAC, or WEGIVEBLUE). The 2024-cycle baseline analysis is anchored to the Palm City cluster composition as of the conduct period; FAIRSHAKE and PROTECT PROGRESS subsequently filed simultaneous amended Form 1 statements on July 7, 2025 moving to 2038 Ford Parkway, St. Paul, MN 55116, while MAJORITY MAKERS PAC, UNITED TOMORROW, and WEGIVEBLUE retained the Palm City registration. The same cryptocurrency-industry donors that fund this committee pipeline lobbied each of the seven federal financial regulators that would administer FIT21's crypto-asset oversight provisions. The upstream pipeline, the within-cluster-pipeline-concentration finding, and the parallel regulator-level advocacy are documented in Exhibit BB.

## 7. The coordination question

Under *Citizens United v. FEC*, 558 U.S. 310 (2010), independent expenditures are constitutionally protected only in the absence of coordination. The regulatory coordination test at 11 C.F.R. § 109.21 reaches material involvement, common vendors, donor-conduit arrangements, and temporal donor-overlap indicia; *McConnell v. FEC*, 540 U.S. 93 (2003), identified donor-universe overlap as probative of coordination sufficient to support Commission inquiry.

The pattern documented above — a single-industry Super PAC whose donors also exhaust the individual contribution limit to the beneficiary's candidate committee, followed within five months by the beneficiary's favorable vote on the industry's signature legislation — presents donor-universe overlap indicia in a form that warrants the opening of a Matter Under Review. Whether PROTECT PROGRESS's independent expenditures were in fact independent of Representative Khanna's campaign is testable through the Federal Election Commission's subpoena authority over communications, shared vendors, and strategic planning.
